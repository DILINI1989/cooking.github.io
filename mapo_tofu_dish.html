Mapo Tofu Dish

 Back to Home
   
  Ingredients:

For the Duck:

4 boneless duck breasts (6-7 oz./170-200g each, with skin)
1/4 tsp salt
1 tsp light soy sauce
1 tsp Shaoxing wine
1/8 tsp five spice powder
1 tbsp oil

For the Mandarin Pancakes:

1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
1/8 tsp salt
2/3 cup boiling water
1 tsp oil

For the Fixings:

1 cucumber (de-seeded, julienned)
1/2 cup cantaloupe (julienned, optional)


Cooking Instructions:

Step1:Marinate the Duck:

Mix salt, soy sauce, wine, and five-spice powder in a bowl.Massage the mixture into duck breasts, leaving them skin side up on a plate.Refrigerate uncovered overnight for optimal marination. (30 minutes if in a hurry)

Step 2 : Prepare Mandarin Pancakes:

Mix flour and salt in a heatproof bowl.Pour boiling water into the mixture and stir until a dough ball forms.Knead the dough for 8 minutes, then let it rest for at least 1 hour.Cut dough into 12 pieces, flatten into discs, and brush 6 with oil.Layer the remaining discs on top, forming 6 pairs.Roll pairs into 7-inch circles, cooking briefly in a pan until air pockets form.Separate the pancakes and keep warm.

Step3 : Cook the Duck and Assemble:

Preheat oven broiler on low.Heat oil in an oven-proof pan over medium-high heat.Sear duck breasts, skin side down, for 6-8 minutes until crispy.Drain off excess duck fat and flip breasts.Transfer to broiler for 3 minutes to finish cooking.Let duck rest for 10-15 minutes, then slice thin.Serve duck with warmed pancakes, fixings, and sauce.

Enjoy your homemade Peking Duck!
